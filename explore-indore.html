Explore Indore

Home >Explore Indore

Rajwada Palace

Built by the Holkars, this seven-story palace is a blend of Maratha and Mughal architecture. While it served as the residence of the Holkar rulers, today it stands as a symbol of the city's rich heritage. Visitors can explore its intricately designed halls, courtyards, and galleries, which showcase artifacts and relics from the past.

Ralamandal Wildlife Sanctuary

Spread across the scenic landscape of the Ralamandal Hills, Ralamandal Wildlife Sanctuary is a haven for nature enthusiasts and wildlife lovers. The sanctuary is home to a diverse array of flora and fauna, including indigenous species of plants, trees, birds, and mammals. Visitors can explore the sanctuary's lush forest trails, go birdwatching, or embark on wildlife safaris to spot animals like deer, monkeys, foxes, and various species of birds in their natural habitat.

Sarafa Bazaar

Located in the heart of Indore, Sarafa Bazaar is a bustling marketplace renowned for its vibrant street food scene. As night falls, the narrow lanes come alive with the aroma of sizzling snacks and the chatter of locals and tourists alike. From crispy kachoris and spicy chaat to delectable sweets like jalebi and rabdi, there's a culinary delight to suit every palate. The lively atmosphere, illuminated by colorful lights and adorned with decorative stalls, makes Sarafa Bazaar a must-visit destination for food lovers.

56 Dukaan

56 Dukaan, also known as Chappan Dukan, is a famous food street located in Indore. The name "Chappan Dukan" translates to "56 shops" in Hindi, and it refers to the row of 56 food stalls lining both sides of the street. This culinary hotspot is a favorite among locals and tourists alike, offering a wide variety of delicious street food options. From savory snacks to sweet treats, visitors to Chappan Dukan can indulge in an array of culinary delights.

Central Museum

Housed in a heritage building, the Central Museum, also known as the Indore Museum, is a treasure trove of artifacts, sculptures, and historical objects that chronicle the rich cultural heritage of the region. The museum's collections span various periods of history, including prehistoric artifacts, archaeological finds, medieval sculptures, and colonial-era memorabilia. Exhibits on art, culture, and natural history provide visitors with insights into the evolution of Indore and its surrounding regions over the centuries.

Patalpani Waterfall

Nestled amidst the lush greenery of the Satpura Range, Patalpani Waterfall is a picturesque natural attraction located a short distance from Indore. The waterfall cascades from a height of around 300 feet into a pool below, creating a mesmerizing sight and a refreshing retreat for nature lovers. Visitors can trek through the surrounding forest trails, picnic by the waterfall, or simply relax and enjoy the serene ambiance of this scenic spot.
